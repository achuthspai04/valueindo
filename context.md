# Valueindo — project context

Read this before doing anything. This is the source of truth for the product.

---

## What is this

Valueindo is an internship scam checker for Indian students — especially tier 2/3 college students who frequently get approached by fake or exploitative internship offers. The name comes from Malayalam slang: "value indo?" = "does it have any value?"

The tool lets a student answer a few structured questions about an internship offer and get back a score, red/green flags, and questions to ask HR before deciding.

---

## Stack

- Next.js (App Router)
- shadcn/ui components

### 2. Form page (`/check`)

Single linear form. No multi-step wizard, no progress bar. Just questions top to bottom, one submit at the bottom.

**Required questions (always shown):**

1. Company or organisation name — free text input
2. How did this opportunity come to you? — radio
   - They messaged me first (WhatsApp / LinkedIn / email)
   - My college gave them my contact
   - I applied through a job platform
   - Someone I know referred me
3. Did they ask you to pay anything? — radio
   - No
   - Yes — to register or apply
   - Yes — to confirm the offer
   - Yes — for training or a kit
   - Yes — something else
4. What is the compensation offered? — radio
   - Fixed monthly stipend
   - Performance or commission based
   - Unpaid
   - Academic credits only
   - Certificate only
   - Not mentioned at all
5. Were you pressured to confirm quickly? — radio
   - Yes — within a few hours
   - Yes — within a day
   - No deadline was given
6. Was there any actual interview or selection process? — radio
   - Yes, a proper interview
   - Just a quick form or quiz
   - No — offer came instantly
   - Not yet, still in process

**Optional questions (hidden under "add more details" toggle):**

7. Did they mention getting your contact from your college? — radio: Yes / No / They implied it
8. Did they provide a formal offer letter or written agreement? — radio: Yes / No / Said it would come later
9. Anything else that felt off? — free text textarea

**Upload section (after optional questions):**

- Label: "Upload offer letter, JD, or screenshots (optional)"
- Accepts: PDF, images, screenshots
- If skipped: show inline nudge — "Uploading even a screenshot helps us catch language patterns and makes your score more accurate."
- Accepted file types: .pdf, .jpg, .jpeg, .png, .webp

**Submit button:** "Analyse this offer" — solid #E8380D

### 3. Result page (`/result/[id]`)

- Score: large number (0–100) with colour — below 40 is #E8380D, 40–70 is amber, above 70 is #2563EB
- Verdict label: "Likely scam" / "Proceed with caution" / "Looks legit"
- Flag pills: 2–3 short red/blue pills pulled from answers
- AI verdict: one sentence generated by Claude
- HR questions: 2–3 questions to ask before accepting
- Two CTAs:
  - "Share this result" — copies the URL to clipboard
  - "Report this company" — opens a simple form that queues a wall submission (stored in DB, not shown publicly yet)

## Scoring logic (rough weights for Claude API prompt)

| Signal | Weight |
|---|---|
| Asked to pay anything | Very high — near-instant scam flag |
| No interview / instant offer | High |
| Certificate or credits only | High |
| They messaged first (cold outreach) | Medium |
| Pressure to confirm fast | Medium |
| College gave contact | Medium |
| No offer letter | Low |

Score 0–100. Higher = more legitimate. Below 35 = "Likely scam". 35–65 = "Proceed with caution". Above 65 = "Looks legit".

## API prompt structure

When calling Claude API, send answers as structured JSON and ask for a response in this JSON shape:

```json
{
  "score": 0-100,
  "verdict": "Likely scam | Proceed with caution | Looks legit",
  "red_flags": ["short flag 1", "short flag 2"],
  "green_flags": ["short flag 1"],
  "verdict_sentence": "One sentence summary of the analysis.",
  "hr_questions": ["Question 1?", "Question 2?", "Question 3?"]
}
```

System prompt should instruct Claude to:
- Be direct and not sugarcoat
- Weight paid-to-confirm and no-interview very heavily
- Consider Indian internship scam patterns specifically
- Keep verdict_sentence under 20 words
- Keep flag text under 6 words each
